Collapse Point

At this stage, the feed can no longer sustain the visual and cultural load placed upon it. Signals collide, overwrite each other, and generate new distortions. The grid becomes an archive of fragments, where the logic that once organised visibility dissolves into interference.

The separation between athlete, spectator, influencer, and algorithm disappears entirely. Every image performs multiple roles at once: documentation, promotion, reaction, and spectacle. The page visualises the moment when these identities merge into a single, unstable media surface.

As repetition, glitches, and overlays intensify, the capacity to interpret the feed breaks down. Performance no longer represents achievement but becomes the raw material of circulation. Collapse Point marks the threshold where the feed stops communicating and begins mutating.

Together, these three stages reveal how contemporary sports culture is inseparable from digital visibility: first curated, then overloaded, and ultimately indistinguishable from the platforms that broadcast it. The project ends here, at the moment where performance, identity, and media converge into the same unstable field.
